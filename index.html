A little about me.

My journey into web development is somewhat unconventional. I earned BAs in Arabic Studies and Cultural Anthropology, and minored in international development. This led me to project management oriented-work after graduating, with some freelance MENA-related consulting on the side. I’m a lifetime learner who has been interested in technology and computers ever since I was introduced to my family’s first Macintosh desktop in the 90’s, where I’d spent hours coming up with new creations in Kid Pix and playing Prince of Persia.

Later on, during high school and college, I dabbled in coding by creating and maintaining multiple personal blogs on Blogger and WordPress. Fast forward to early 2016 when I began taking beginner’s Python classes with Hear Me Code, which was my formal introdution to the #DCTech community. I adore the structured-creativity that comes along with code, and with my growing web development skill-set, I’m ready to turn web designs into beautiful and pixel-perfect code.

Hand-Coded HTML

My focus is writing clean, well-formatted, semantic HTML5 by hand to make sure that the content is easy to read, collaborate, trouble-shoot and is accessible.

Well-Organized CSS

I pride myself on writing CSS that is easy to read and build on. I focus on keeping my CSS lean and fast to load, and I make a habit of staying up to date on best practices.

Ease Converting Designs into Code

You can trust me to take a designer’s PSD and quickly & accurately convert it into a webpage that is pixel-perfect match.
